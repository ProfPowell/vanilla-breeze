The alpine meadows above the treeline host an extraordinary diversity of wildflowers during the brief summer season. Snow gentians, edelweiss, and alpine asters compete for the attention of pollinators in an environment where the growing window lasts only a few weeks. Botanists have catalogued over two hundred species in a single hectare of undisturbed meadow, making these high-altitude grasslands some of the most species-rich habitats in temperate Europe.
