Origins of Movable Type

Movable type was first developed in China around 1040 AD by Bi Sheng, who used ceramic pieces to compose pages of text. The technique spread westward over the following centuries.

Gutenberg's Press

Johannes Gutenberg independently invented movable type in Europe around 1440, using a metal alloy that produced durable, reusable letters. His printing press transformed the speed and economics of book production.

Spread Across Europe

By 1500, an estimated twenty million volumes had been printed across Europe, making books affordable for the first time in history.

The Typographic Tradition

The centuries following Gutenberg saw an explosion of typeface design, from the humanist serifs of Aldus Manutius to the geometric sans-serifs of the early twentieth century.
