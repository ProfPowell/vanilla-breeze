The morning light crept through the shutters, casting thin stripes across the wooden floor. She sat at the kitchen table, turning a cold cup of coffee in her hands, listening to the house settle around her.

Outside, the first birds had begun to call. It was the kind of quiet that felt intentional, as though the world itself were holding its breath before the day began.

She stood, finally, and crossed to the window. The garden was still wet with dew, each leaf carrying a small bright light. It would be a good day, she decided. It had to be.
