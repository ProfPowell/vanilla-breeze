Shipping

How long does shipping take?

Standard shipping takes 5–7 business days within the continental US. Express shipping delivers in 2–3 business days. Overnight shipping arrives the next business day for orders placed before 2pm MT. Alaska and Hawaii orders should add 2–3 additional business days. International orders typically arrive within 7–14 business days depending on destination and customs processing.

How much does shipping cost?

Standard shipping is free on all orders over $75. Orders under $75 have a flat $7.95 shipping fee. Express shipping is $12.99, and overnight is $24.99 regardless of order size. International shipping rates are calculated at checkout based on destination and package weight.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes! We currently ship to Canada, the UK, EU countries, Australia, New Zealand, and Japan. International orders may be subject to local customs duties and taxes, which are the responsibility of the recipient. Delivery typically takes 7–14 business days.

Can I track my order?

Absolutely. Once your order ships, you’ll receive an email with a tracking number and a link to track your package in real time. You can also check your order status anytime at our Order Status page.

What carriers do you use?

We primarily ship via USPS for standard orders and UPS for express and overnight shipments. International orders ship via DHL or UPS depending on the destination. We select the most reliable carrier for each route to ensure your gear arrives safely.

Do you offer in-store pickup?

Yes, if you’re near our Boulder, Colorado flagship store, you can select “Store Pickup” at checkout. Orders are typically ready within 2 hours during business hours. We’ll send you an email when your order is ready for collection.

Returns & Exchanges

What is your return policy?

We offer a 60-day, no-questions-asked return policy on all unused gear in original condition. Used gear can be returned within 30 days if it doesn’t meet your expectations — we’d rather you love your gear than keep something that doesn’t work for you.

How do I start a return?

Contact us at returns@alpenglowgear.example or call (303) 555-0142. We’ll email you a prepaid shipping label. Pack the item in its original packaging if possible, attach the label, and drop it off at any UPS location. Refunds are processed within 5 business days of receiving the return.

Can I exchange an item instead of returning it?

Yes. Just mention the item you’d like instead when you contact us. If there’s a price difference, we’ll charge or refund the difference. Exchanges ship free.

Do you accept returns on sale items?

Yes, all sale items are eligible for return within 30 days. The refund will be for the price you paid, not the original retail price.

What if my item arrives damaged?

Contact us immediately with photos of the damage. We’ll ship a replacement at no cost and arrange free pickup of the damaged item. No return needed — just let us make it right.

Products

Are your products waterproof?

It depends on the product. Our Trail Runner Pack and Summit Shelter both use waterproof materials with sealed seams and carry IPX4 or higher ratings. The Beacon Headlamp is rated IPX8 (submersible to 2 meters). Check individual product specs for exact ratings. Remember that waterproof coatings require periodic maintenance — see our Care & Maintenance section below.

What materials do you use?

We primarily use high-tenacity ripstop nylon (210D for packs, 20D for tent bodies) with silicone and PU coatings for waterproofing. Hardware is anodized aluminum and YKK zippers throughout. Our tent poles are DAC Featherlite NSL — the same premium alloy used by the world’s top tent makers.

How do I find the right size?

Each product page includes a detailed sizing chart. For backpacks, measure your torso length (C7 vertebra to iliac crest). For tents, consider both the number of occupants and their heights — our floor dimensions account for sleepers up to 6’2” in the 3P model.

Do you sell replacement parts?

Yes. We stock replacement parts for all current and recent products — zippers, buckles, poles, stakes, stuff sacks, and more. Contact us with your product name and the part you need, and we’ll get it to you, often at no charge under our Lifetime Repair Promise.

Can I see your products in person?

Visit our flagship store in Boulder, Colorado, or check our website for upcoming trade shows and demo events. We attend major outdoor shows in Salt Lake City, Denver, and Portland, where you can see and test everything hands-on.

Care & Maintenance

How do I clean my backpack?

Empty all pockets and shake out debris. For spot cleaning, use a damp cloth with mild soap. For deep cleaning, fill a bathtub with lukewarm water, add a technical fabric wash (like Nikwax Tech Wash), and gently scrub with a soft brush. Rinse thoroughly and air dry completely. Never machine wash a structured pack — it can damage the frame and padding.

How often should I re-waterproof my gear?

Check your DWR (Durable Water Repellent) coating regularly. If water stops beading on the surface and starts soaking in, it’s time to reapply. For rain jackets, this is typically every 10–15 washes. For tents, check at the start of each season. We recommend Nikwax TX.Direct for most applications.

How should I store my tent?

Never store a tent compressed in its stuff sack for extended periods. Instead, loosely fold or stuff it into a large breathable bag (we include one with every tent). Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Most importantly, make sure it’s completely dry before storage — even slight dampness can lead to mildew and coating breakdown.

What if a zipper gets stuck?

First, check for fabric caught in the teeth. Gently work any caught material free. If the zipper is just stiff, apply a zipper lubricant (we recommend McNett Zip Care). Run the slider back and forth several times. If the slider itself is worn and not closing the teeth properly, it’s time for a replacement — contact us for a free repair under our Lifetime Repair Promise.

Can I machine wash my tent?

No. Machine washing can damage waterproof coatings, tear mesh panels, and stress seams. Always hand wash tents. Set up the tent in your yard, rinse with a hose, then spot clean with a sponge and tent-specific cleaner. Rinse again and let it air dry completely before packing away.

How do I repair a small tear in my tent or pack?

For field repairs, Tenacious Tape is your best friend. Clean the area around the tear, cut a patch larger than the tear with rounded corners (sharp corners peel), and press firmly. For permanent repairs, send the item to our repair center — we’ll fix it professionally at no charge.

Warranty

What does the Lifetime Repair Promise cover?

Our Lifetime Repair Promise covers all manufacturing defects and normal wear for the original purchaser. This includes seam failures, zipper defects, delamination, buckle breakage, and pole failures. It does not cover damage from misuse, modifications, or accidents — but even then, we’ll repair it for a reasonable fee.

How long is the warranty period?

The Lifetime Repair Promise lasts for the usable life of the product for the original purchaser. There’s no arbitrary time limit. If your pack’s seam fails after 10 years of regular use, we’ll fix it.

How do I make a warranty claim?

Email warranty@alpenglowgear.example with your name, order number (if available), product name, and a description and photos of the issue. We’ll respond within 2 business days with repair instructions or a prepaid shipping label. Most repairs are completed and returned within 10 business days.

Is the warranty transferable?

The Lifetime Repair Promise applies to the original purchaser only. However, second-hand owners can still get repairs at our cost — we charge a fair price for parts and labor, but we’ll never turn away an Alpenglow product that needs fixing.

Do you cover shipping for warranty repairs?

Yes. We provide prepaid shipping labels for all warranty claims within the US and Canada. International warranty shipping is subsidized — you pay a flat $15 fee regardless of location.
