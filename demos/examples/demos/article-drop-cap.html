The Art of Typography on the Web

February 24, 2026

Typography has always been the foundation of good design. Long before the web existed, typographers spent centuries perfecting the craft of setting type — choosing faces, adjusting spacing, and creating visual hierarchies that guide the reader through content.

On the web, we have finally caught up. Modern CSS provides tools that rival traditional typesetting: variable fonts, optical sizing, balanced text wrapping, and fine-grained control over every aspect of how text appears on screen.

The drop cap you see at the start of this article is one such typographic convention. Used since medieval manuscripts, it signals the beginning of a new piece and draws the eye to the opening words.
