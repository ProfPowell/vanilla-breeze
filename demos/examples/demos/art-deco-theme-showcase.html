Art Deco

Gold, black, and cream inspired by 1920s luxury design. Geometric serif typography, sharp corners, and directional shadows evoke the elegance of the Jazz Age.

Typography & Content

Art Deco uses geometric serif typography for headings with uppercase letterspacing. The sharp zero-radius corners and gold-tinted hard shadows create a distinctive architectural feel.

The Art Deco movement emerged in the 1920s as a celebration of modernity, luxury, and geometric beauty. It influenced architecture, fashion, and graphic design with its bold lines and metallic accents.

Links use the signature gold color with a subtle underline that strengthens on hover. The deep teal accent provides contrast for secondary actions.

Accordion: Design Influences

This theme draws from the Chrysler Building, the posters of Tamara de Lempicka, and the geometric patterns found in Art Deco architecture. Gold leaf, stepped forms, and sunburst motifs define the visual language.

Accordion: Modern Application

Art Deco translates beautifully to web design through sharp geometric shapes, metallic color accents, and strong typographic hierarchy. The theme proves that century-old design principles remain compelling in digital interfaces.
