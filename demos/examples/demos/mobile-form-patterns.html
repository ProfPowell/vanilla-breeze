Mobile Form Patterns

Side-by-side comparisons of what most sites do wrong vs what they should do. Every pattern uses native HTML — no libraries needed.

1. Phone Number Input

The correct type triggers the right keyboard on mobile.

What most sites do

Uses type="text" — shows full QWERTY keyboard. User hunts for numbers.

What you should do

Uses type="tel" — triggers numeric dialpad. autocomplete="tel" enables one-tap autofill.

2. Credit Card Number

Never use type="number" for cards — spinners appear and values get truncated.

What most sites do

type="number" adds spinner arrows, drops leading zeros, truncates long numbers. No autofill.

What you should do

inputmode="numeric" shows number pad without spinners. autocomplete="cc-number" triggers card scan on iOS.

3. Email Autocomplete

Proper autocomplete enables one-tap form filling from saved data.

What most sites do

Wrong type, no autocomplete. Browser can't help. User types the full address.

What you should do

type="email" shows @ keyboard. autocomplete="email" enables one-tap fill. enterkeyhint="next" shows "Next" key.

4. Validation Timing

Validate on blur (when user leaves), not on input (while typing).

What most sites do

Validates on every keystroke. Shows "invalid email" after typing one letter. Feels aggressive.

What you should do

Validates on blur — waits until the user finishes typing and leaves the field. Respectful and clear.

5. Country Selection

For long lists, <datalist> lets users search. <select> is fine for short lists.

What most sites do

A <select> with 200+ options. User scrolls endlessly on the native picker. No way to search.

What you should do

<datalist> gives searchable suggestions. User types "Un" and sees United States + United Kingdom. Zero JS.

6. Label Placement

Placeholder-as-label disappears when typing. Real labels stay visible.

What most sites do

Placeholder disappears on focus. User forgets what the field is for mid-typing. No accessible label.

What you should do

Real <label> above the field — always visible. Placeholder is a hint, not the label.
