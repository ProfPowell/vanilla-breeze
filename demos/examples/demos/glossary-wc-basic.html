Acceptance Criteria
Conditions that a product or feature must satisfy to be accepted by the product owner or stakeholders.
Backlog
An ordered list of work items that need to be completed, typically maintained by the product owner.
Epic
A large body of work that can be broken down into smaller user stories. See also: velocity, sprint.
Sprint
A fixed time period (usually 1-4 weeks) during which a team works to complete a set of committed backlog items.
Velocity
The amount of work a team can complete in a single sprint, measured in story points or hours.
API Gateway
Single entry point for all client requests, handling routing, authentication, and rate limiting.
CI/CD
Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery — automated processes for building, testing, and deploying code changes.
Microservice
A small, independently deployable service that focuses on a single business capability and communicates via APIs.
Definition of Done
A shared understanding of what it means for work to be complete, including coding, testing, and documentation standards.
Retrospective
A meeting held after a sprint to reflect on what went well, what could be improved, and what actions to take going forward.
