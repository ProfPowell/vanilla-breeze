Desert Dunes

Deserts cover about one-third of Earth's land surface, yet they're far from empty. These landscapes of extremes host remarkable life forms that have adapted to survive with minimal water and punishing temperatures.

The sculpted dunes shift with the wind, creating an ever-changing canvas of light and shadow. At night, the clear skies reveal a tapestry of stars invisible from light-polluted cities.

The desert teaches patience and resilience, showing how life finds a way even in the harshest conditions.
