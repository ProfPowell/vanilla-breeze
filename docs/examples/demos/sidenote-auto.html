The Printing Revolution

Gutenberg's printing press transformed European society.Johannes Gutenberg developed movable type around 1440 in Mainz, Germany. Before its invention, books were copied by hand in monasteries — a single volume could take months to produce. The press reduced that to days.

The impact on literacy was dramatic.Literacy rates in Europe rose from roughly 10% in 1450 to over 50% by 1800, though the press was one of many contributing factors. Affordable printed material meant that knowledge was no longer confined to the clergy and aristocracy. Pamphlets, broadsheets, and eventually newspapers created a new public sphere for debate.

Martin Luther's 95 Theses spread across Germany within weeks.Published in 1517. An estimated 300,000 copies were in circulation within three years. Without the press, the Reformation might have remained a local theological dispute. Instead, it became a continent-wide movement that reshaped politics, religion, and culture for centuries.
