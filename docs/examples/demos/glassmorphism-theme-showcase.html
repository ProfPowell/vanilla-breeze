Glassmorphism

Frosted glass surfaces with backdrop blur, translucent layers, and gradient mesh backgrounds. iOS-inspired aesthetics meet web design.

Typography & Content

Glassmorphism uses clean Inter-style typography with light weights — glass surfaces need breathing room. The large border radius creates pill-shaped buttons and rounded cards.

The frosted glass effect layers translucent surfaces over a colorful gradient background. Each surface uses backdrop-filter blur to create depth through transparency rather than shadow.

Links use a saturated blue-purple primary that stands out against the translucent surfaces. Motion is smooth and slightly floaty.

Accordion: Glass Design Principles

Glassmorphism creates depth through translucency rather than traditional elevation shadows. Surfaces at different layers use varying opacity levels — higher surfaces are more opaque, creating a natural depth hierarchy through the glass metaphor.

Accordion: The Gradient Background

The gradient mesh background uses multiple radial gradients in purple, blue, and teal. This colorful backdrop shines through the frosted surfaces, creating visual interest that changes based on scroll position and surface placement.
